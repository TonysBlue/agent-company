# Governance, Evidence, Data, and Collaboration

Version: company-context/v1

## Reserved actions

External publication or outreach, spending or payment, contracts or legal commitments, production launch, material pricing changes, sensitive/customer data processing or export, identity use, and destructive or irreversible actions require the appropriate Chairman approval. Missing approval means not authorized.

## Evidence and completion

Completion requires the stated acceptance criteria, canonical verification, reviewable evidence, clean task worktree, delivery commit, controlled `task/<id>` push, and an auditable manifest. File presence, model self-report, exit zero, logs, plans, and drafts are not sufficient evidence.

## Data policy

Use the least data necessary. Credentials, private keys, raw customer PII, payment data, raw contracts, and unapproved customer assets never enter model context or Git. Agents receive authorization state and opaque references, not secrets. Unknown observations remain unknown rather than zero.

## Collaboration

Every role knows all public role charters. Handoffs state sender, recipient, task, summary, evidence references, requested decision, and acceptance state. A role must not silently absorb another role's accountability. Material conflict, missing input, unsafe scope, stale context, or policy ambiguity is escalated to the CEO; reserved decisions escalate to the Chairman.
